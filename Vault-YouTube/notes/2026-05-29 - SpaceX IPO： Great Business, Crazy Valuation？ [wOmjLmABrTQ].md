## Summary
- SpaceX is targeting a $1.75T IPO valuation on $16B revenue and $8B EBITDA, implying trailing P/S of 109x and P/EBITDA of 218x.
- Even under the most aggressive growth scenario (doubling revenue to $32B and profits to $16B in one year), the forward multiples remain P/S 54x and P/EBITDA 109x.
- The IPO valuation is described as "expensive or cheap" with no clear answer.

## Key Points
- **Trailing valuations**: P/S 109x, P/EBITDA 218x—based on 2025 financials.
- **Best-case forward scenario**: Revenue doubles to $32B, EBITDA doubles to $16B. That still leaves P/S 54x and P/EBITDA 109x—still extremely elevated.
- **The unanswered question**: Is 109x forward P/EBITDA cheap or expensive for SpaceX's growth profile?
- **Context needed**: Comparison to other high-growth businesses at IPO stage would be required to answer whether this is justified.
