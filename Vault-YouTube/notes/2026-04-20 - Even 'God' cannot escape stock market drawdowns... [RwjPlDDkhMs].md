## Summary
- Researcher Wes Gray's article "Even God Would Get Fired" showed that even a portfolio with perfect omniscient knowledge of the next 5 years' best performers would still suffer a 76% drawdown and five 30%+ drawdowns.
- This proves that drawdowns are an unavoidable structural feature of equity ownership—not evidence of being wrong.
- The implication: if you're diversified and holding quality companies, temporary drops are expected, not alarming.

## Key Points
- **The research**: Build a portfolio where you know in advance which stocks will perform best over the next 5 years. Even this perfect portfolio suffers massive drawdowns.
- **76% maximum drawdown**: The omniscient portfolio would have lost 76% at peak-to-trough on its way to beating the market.
- **Five separate 30% drawdowns**: Even while correctly picking the best stocks, temporary declines of 30%+ happen repeatedly.
- **The implication for investors**: Drawdowns aren't signals to sell or that your thesis is wrong—they are the *price you pay* for long-term outperformance.
- **Contrarian investing requires this tolerance**: Buying undervalued stocks (by definition on downtrends) means your portfolio will drop more than the market before it recovers.

## Notable Quotes
> "Drawdowns aren't evidence that you're wrong. They are a structural feature of equity ownership."
