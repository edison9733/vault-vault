## Summary
- SpaceX is targeting a mid-2026 IPO at $1.75–2T valuation—the largest in history.
- The structure is unusual: 66% primary shares (money goes to company), 33% vendor/secondary shares (money goes to insiders).
- SpaceX is allocating ~30% to retail investors—three times the typical retail allocation of 10%.

## Key Points
- **Largest IPO ever**: $1.75–2T valuation; $75B total raise proposed.
- **Primary shares (66%)**: ~$50B goes into the company for operations/expansion.
- **Vendor/secondary shares (33%)**: ~$25B goes to insiders and employees who are exiting.
- **Retail allocation (22.5% of $75B = ~$22.5B)**: Three times larger than typical IPO retail tranche—unusually generous.
- **The cynical read**: Are they doing this because they can't attract enough institutional buyers, so they're targeting retail investors who may be "less sophisticated"?
- **The generous read**: Elon Musk and management genuinely want retail investors to participate.
