## Summary
- Dennis, a UC Berkeley CS 2022 graduate, shares his experience joining Oracle (NetSuite division) and being laid off after 3.5 years.
- Oracle compensation structure: $120K base + $20K sign-on + $10K relocation + ~$40K/year in stock (bought at $60/share, peaked near $320).
- The day of layoff was dehumanizing: denied building access to collect belongings, surrounded by coworkers leaving.

## Key Points
- **Oracle vs startup choice**: Dennis chose Oracle for stability and reputation over a startup offer; regrets nothing for the experience.
- **Job search (2022 vs now)**: Got offer in March 2022; considered hard at the time but now (2026) the job market is significantly worse.
- **What Oracle/NetSuite does**: OCI (Oracle Cloud Infrastructure) competes with AWS; NetSuite is enterprise B2B accounting software (like AWS for mid-market business operations).
- **Day-to-day reality**: Mostly maintaining legacy code rather than building new features. Expected for large enterprise companies.
- **Tech debt**: Significant, because the company scaled from small to large and early systems weren't built for scale.
- **Comp trajectory**: First year ~$190K total; third year ~$280K (stock appreciated 4-5x from $60 to $200-320).
- **Promotions at Oracle**: Slow. Got promoted once in 3.5 years (typical for large enterprise).
- **LeetCode opinion**: Not a good reflection of engineering ability; interviewers should care about problem-solving approach and collaboration style.
- **Layoff day**: Security prevented entry to the building; couldn't say goodbye to coworkers or collect belongings. "Dehumanizing."

## Notable Quotes
> "I worked at this company for so long and I'm not able to even go to my desk and say goodbye to any of my co-workers."
