## Summary
- The "big crash" people are waiting for has already happened—three times in 10 years (COVID 2020: -35%, 2022: -27%, 2025 tariff war: -20%)—and recovered within months each time.
- Modern market structure means crashes are shorter and more frequent but far less prolonged than historical examples (Great Depression: -89% over 3 years; dot-com: -48% over 2.5 years).
- The lesson: stop waiting on the sidelines; time in the market beats timing the market.

## Key Points
- **Prophets of doom cycle**: Harry Dent predicted crashes every year from 2008–2017; was eventually right (broken clock). Those who listened missed 790% compound gains over 16 years.
- **S&P stats**: Staying invested through all crashes from 2016–2026 = **311% gain**. Buying dips = 400-700% gain.
- **Why crashes are shorter now**:
  1. **The Fed**: Actively pumps liquidity (rate cuts, QE, bond buying) at the first sign of trouble. COVID: cut to zero in months, pumped $5T (vs $1.2T during GFC).
  2. **Speed of information**: Markets price in news in minutes/hours, not weeks.
  3. **High-frequency trading**: Algorithms react and correct faster than human traders.
- **Modern market reality**: Many more bear markets (3 in 10 years vs historical average 1 every 6 years), but each one recovers in months, not years.
- **The Great Depression comparison trap**: People waiting for a multi-year decline will never get in.
- **The real risk**: Not being in the market means purchasing power erodes from inflation over decades.
