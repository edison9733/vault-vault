## Summary
- S&P 500 is down ~9% YTD while the author's portfolio is down more than 9% due to no energy exposure.
- Energy stocks are buoying the index because of high oil prices, while tech/growth names (Microsoft, Nvidia, Meta, Palo Alto) are down double digits.
- This is described as a "bizarro period" where index performance doesn't reflect what growth-oriented investors actually hold.

## Key Points
- **S&P 500 YTD**: Down ~9%.
- **Author's portfolio**: Down more than 9% because concentrated in sectors that are down harder (tech, healthcare, communication, consumer cyclical).
- **Energy sector as S&P prop**: Oil price rise → energy companies profit → energy stocks up → they hold up the index.
- **Growth stocks suffering**: Microsoft, Nvidia, Meta, Palo Alto all down double digits YTD.
- **The "bizarro" disconnect**: Index shows -9% but most growth investor portfolios show -20% or more. The S&P 500 is not a clean benchmark during sector rotations.
- **Sector conviction**: Author stays in these sectors because long-term, higher growth + economic moats + higher ROC outperform energy/commodity stocks.
