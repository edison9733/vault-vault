## Summary
- Anatoly Yakovenko (Solana co-founder) argues Solana is the infrastructure of open, global finance, having processed over $1 trillion in volume last year.
- Major traditional finance trading firms are now building on Solana rather than competing with it, treating the chain as the lowest-latency settlement layer available.
- Yakovenko predicts DeFi and traditional finance will fully converge within 10 years, and that the final competitive frontier in trading is not other firms but physics itself.

## Key Points
- **[[Anatoly Yakovenko]]** co-founded [[Solana]] with a fully open, permissionless finance vision — "complete control over the entire stack," analogous to [[Linux]] as shared open infrastructure.
- **$1 trillion** in volume processed by Solana in 2025; network runs 24/7 with all code open-source.
- **Traditional finance adoption**: large trading firms are switching from competing with crypto to building on-chain on Solana, drawn by throughput and latency advantages over legacy venues.
- **[[DeFi]] convergence**: "There's not going to be a difference between DeFi and finance. We'll just call it all finance." — the on-chain vs. off-chain split dissolves as liquidity migrates to the faster venue.
- ==**Physics as the final competitive frontier**: at the limit of high-frequency trading, "the only enemy left isn't a competitor. You are fighting physics." Speed of light and network latency become the binding constraint, not rival firms.==
