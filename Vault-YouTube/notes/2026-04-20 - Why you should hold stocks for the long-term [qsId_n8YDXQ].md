## Summary
- Amazon's history is used as a case study for why long-term conviction matters: it dropped 95% in 2001, 56% in 2022, and 33% in 2024–25, yet has compounded 258,000% in total.
- Holding through every crash would have turned a small investment into extraordinary wealth.
- The lesson: "You need to be a man of focus, of commitment, of sheer will" to hold great companies through their worst periods.

## Key Points
- **Amazon's gut-wrenching crashes**:
  - 1999–2001: -95% (dot-com crash); now worth $2.14T
  - 2022: -56%
  - 2024–25: -33% (tariff war), twice
- **Total compound gain**: 258,000% vs S&P 500's 567%
- **The holding discipline required**: Watching an investment fall 95% while still believing in the business is psychologically brutal—but it's the price of owning a compounding machine.
- **Comparison**: S&P 500 returned 567% over the same period—impressive but 450x less than holding Amazon through the pain.
- **The wealth creation formula**: Find great businesses, buy when undervalued, hold through the crashes.

## Notable Quotes
> "You need to be a man of focus, of commitment, of sheer will to hold a good company to get the good return."
