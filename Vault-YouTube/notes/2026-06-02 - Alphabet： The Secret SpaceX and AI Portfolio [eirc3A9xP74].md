## Summary
- Alphabet holds a hidden portfolio of high-growth private companies that are not reflected in its share price, including 7% of SpaceX and 14% of Anthropic.
- Additional holdings include Waymo (leading autonomous driving), Stripe, AST Space Mobile, and Planet Labs.
- Buying Alphabet effectively gives investors exposure to these private assets for free on top of Google's core business.

## Key Points
- **Alphabet as Berkshire of tech**: Many great tech companies—both public and private—are owned by Alphabet's venture portfolio.
- **SpaceX stake**: Alphabet invested $900M in 2015 for a 7% stake. SpaceX pre-IPO valuation was $87–140B, making the holding worth roughly $6–10B—none of which is priced into Alphabet shares.
- **Anthropic stake**: Alphabet owns 14% of Anthropic, creator of the Claude LLM, which is disrupting SaaS and enterprise software. Also not priced in.
- **Waymo**: World's leading autonomous driving company; Tesla is still trying to catch up.
- **AST Space Mobile and Planet Labs**: Other significant positions in the emerging **space economy**.
- **The free lunch argument**: When you buy Alphabet stock, you get Google's search/cloud/ads business plus these hidden assets at no extra cost.
