## Summary
- SpaceX is targeting a $1.75–2T valuation for its IPO, the same price as Amazon's current public market cap.
- The fundamental difference: Amazon has $716B in revenue and $146B in current profits vs SpaceX's $16B revenue and $8B EBITDA.
- At identical valuations, Amazon trades at P/S 3x and P/EBITDA 16x; SpaceX would trade at P/S 109x and P/EBITDA 218x.

## Key Points
- **The $2 trillion thought experiment**: If you could buy either company outright for $2T, which would you choose?
- **Amazon's current financials**: $716B revenue, $146B profits, trading at P/S 3x, P/EBITDA 16x—considered reasonable valuation.
- **SpaceX's current financials**: $16B revenue, $8B EBITDA, targeting P/S **109x** and P/EBITDA **218x** at the $2T ask.
- **The multiple gap is staggering**: SpaceX would need to be valued 36x more per unit of revenue than Amazon to justify the same $2T price.
- **Growth potential**: SpaceX could grow rapidly, but the bar to justify current multiples is extremely high.
