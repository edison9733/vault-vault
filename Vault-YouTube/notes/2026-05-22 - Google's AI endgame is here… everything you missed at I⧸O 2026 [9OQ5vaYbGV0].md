## Summary
- Google I/O 2026 declared the **agentic Gemini era** — Gemini is now embedded in Search, Gmail, Android, and AR glasses, repositioning Google as the universal interface to reality rather than a search engine.
- Infrastructure growth is staggering: Google went from 9.7 trillion to 3.2 quadrillion tokens/month in two years, enabled by new specialized **TPU-T** (training) and **TPU-I** (inference) chips.
- Key launches include [[Gemini Omni]] (fully multimodal world model), **Gemini Flash 3.5** (fast but 30× pricier than Gemini 1.5 Flash), the **Anti-gravity IDE** (agent management over code writing), and the **HTML on Canvas API** in Chrome.

## Key Points

- **Agentic Gemini era**: Google's stated endgame — [[Gemini]] injected into every product (Search, Gmail, Android, glasses); roadmap is "take Gemini, append a noun, ship it."
- **Scale**: 9.7 trillion → 3.2 quadrillion tokens/month in two years; Alphabet capex exploding to build supporting infrastructure.
- **[[TPU]] specialization**: Split into TPU-T (optimized for training) and TPU-I (optimized for inference) — moving away from a single general-purpose chip.
- **[[Gemini Omni]]**: flagship natively multimodal model (any input — text, video, sound — to any output); Demis Hassabis described as "world model pilled" — the model understands language, physics, and motion well enough to simulate reality on demand.
- **Neural Expressive**: new Gemini app design system that generates UI elements (diagrams, timelines, mini-apps) on demand rather than displaying static results.
- **Gemini Flash 3.5**: fast model benchmarked near Opus 4.7 and GPT-5.5; **3× pricier** than Flash 3.0, **30× pricier** than Gemini 1.5 Flash — speed gains come at a cost.
- **Gemini 3.5 Pro**: flagship model still unreleased; expected later summer 2026, disappointing many.
- **[[AI Coding IDE]] — Anti-gravity** (formerly Windserve): AI IDE shifting from code-writing toward **agent management**, similar to OpenAI Codex; live demo built a full OS from scratch (~12 hours, billions of tokens) and fixed missing Doom drivers live on stage.
- **[[HTML on Canvas API]]**: new Chrome API allowing native HTML elements to render directly in a canvas, enabling pixel-perfect UIs that combine WebGL/WebGPU control with standard HTML components.
- Google's competitive framing: race against [[Anthropic]] and [[OpenAI]] to own the "interface to reality" before they build "better realities."
