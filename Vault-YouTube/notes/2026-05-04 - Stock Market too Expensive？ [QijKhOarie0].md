## Summary
- Warren Buffett holding $397B cash with "nothing to buy" signals expensive valuations—but only in his narrow circle of competence (old-economy stocks), not in the broader market.
- Stocks like Costco (52x PE), Walmart (48x PE), and Coca-Cola (25x PE) are genuinely expensive by DCF analysis.
- Meanwhile, tech stocks like Meta ($608 price vs $749 intrinsic value) and Microsoft ($418 vs $553) remain undervalued.

## Key Points
- **Buffett's philosophy**: Only invest in businesses within his circle of competence. Never market-times; holds cash only when he can't find undervalued quality companies.
- **Buffett's blind spot**: Tech companies. He only uses a flip phone to make calls and has no computer on his desk. He bought IBM and lost money (too complex). Apple was an exception—he views it as a consumer utility, not tech.
- **Where Buffett fishes**: Old-economy companies (railroads, insurance, industrials). These ARE overvalued: Costco at 52x PE (intrinsic value ~$488, price ~$1,000), Walmart at 48x PE (intrinsic value ~$39, price $131), Coca-Cola at 25x PE.
- **Where the value is**: Tech stocks that Buffett avoids. Example: Meta—intrinsic value $749, current price $608 (undervalued even accounting for elevated CapEx from AI data center spending).
- **The fishing analogy**: Buffett fishes in shallow water (overvalued old economy). The deep water (tech) has plenty of fish.
- **PE ratio limitation**: PE alone is misleading; need to look at growth rates, forward PE, and DCF analysis. A company growing 30% at 50x PE can be cheap; a company at 25x PE growing 5% can be expensive.

## Notable Quotes
> "Buffett only fishes at certain parts of the ocean and I fish at certain parts. The ocean isn't all shallow."
> "Stock market is rigged in your favor... The market is rigged to always go up."
