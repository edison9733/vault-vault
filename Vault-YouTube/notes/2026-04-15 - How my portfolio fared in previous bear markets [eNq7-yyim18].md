## Summary
- The author's 7-year portfolio (Jan 2019–Jan 2026) returned 267% vs S&P 500's 175%, but experienced worse drawdowns than the index.
- Major drawdowns: -36% (COVID 2020), -26% (2022 Fed rate hikes), -19% (2025 tariff war).
- Portfolio is concentrated in healthcare, tech, financials, communication services, and consumer cyclicals—avoiding energy stocks explains the larger-than-S&P drawdowns.

## Key Points
- **Outperformance**: 267% vs 175% (S&P) over 7 years.
- **Drawdowns larger than index**: S&P was only down ~9% in the recent tariff war period, but the author was down ~19% because of no energy holdings.
- **Energy as buffer**: Energy stocks held up the S&P index during the tariff war while tech/growth sold off hard.
- **Portfolio sectors**: Healthcare, technology, financial stocks, communication services, consumer discretionary—all higher growth, higher beta.
- **Why accept this**: Higher growth rates, stronger economic moats, better return on capital vs S&P components.
- **Double-digit drops are normal**: Even holding top-quality companies means accepting 20–30%+ drawdowns in bear markets.
