## Summary
- Anatoly Yakovenko, Solana co-founder, explains his vision: Solana as completely open finance where anyone can see and control the entire stack—like Linux for financial infrastructure.
- His frustration with trading via Interactive Brokers (black box, opaque order routing) inspired building a "global state machine" with full transparency.
- Key claim: multiple concurrent proposers (eliminating leader monopoly) means Solana can process orders from Singapore and New York simultaneously—beating TradFi's geographic latency.

## Key Points
- **Open finance vision**: "It's completely open finance—full control over the entire stack." Processed over $1 trillion in transactions in the past year; runs 24/7.
- **Black box problem with TradFi**: When trading traditional markets, you can't see how your order gets from broker to exchange. Solana exposes the entire pipeline.
- **Physics as the competitive frontier**: "In this game, the only enemy left isn't a competitor. You are fighting physics."
- **Multiple concurrent proposers**: Solana's new consensus mechanism eliminates the single block leader's temporary monopoly. Orders from Singapore and New York can be processed in the same "economic tick."
- **The geographic latency advantage**: A ship sinking in Singapore harbor affects Apple's stock in New York—but by the time the news wire reaches NYSE, Solana has already priced it in.
- **DeFi becoming finance**: "There's not going to be a difference between DeFi and finance. We'll just call it all finance."
- **Linux analogy**: Solana's goal is to be the Linux of finance—adopted by big firms (like Intel, Google adopted Linux) because a common open standard beats proprietary alternatives.
- **Fire Dancer + Alpenglow**: Jump Trading built Fire Dancer (alternative validator client); Alpenglow (new consensus by Anza) brings block finality to ~150ms.
- **Traditional trading desks going 24/7**: All major traditional finance is already moving to 24/7 trading, converging on crypto's always-on model.
- **Fairness as finance's core**: "A lot of finance is really all about fairness. Open source protocols just increase transparency and improve fairness."
