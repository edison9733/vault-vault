## Summary
- The **Musk vs. Altman** federal trial in Oakland reached closing arguments, with trial discovery revealing the full 11-year arc of OpenAI's transformation from nonprofit to near-$1 trillion for-profit entity.
- Key evidence shows Musk himself pushed in 2017 to convert OpenAI to for-profit with himself as CEO — fatally undermining his claim that the conversion was a betrayal of the original mission.
- [[Polymarket]] gives Elon a 32% chance of winning; the creator assesses it as lower still, given Musk admitted under oath that [[xAI]]/[[Grok]] stole [[OpenAI]] models.

## Key Points

- **Founding (2015)**: [[Elon Musk]], [[Sam Altman]], Greg Brockman, and Ilya Sutskever co-founded [[OpenAI]] as a nonprofit to benefit humanity. Musk donated $38M, then tried to seize control, failed, rage-quit the board in 2018, and founded [[xAI]].
- **The pivot**: OpenAI bolted a for-profit subsidiary onto the nonprofit, took **$13 billion from [[Microsoft]]**, and is now approaching a ~**$1 trillion valuation**.
- **Musk's claims**: disgorgement of up to **$134 billion**, unwind the for-profit conversion, remove Altman and Brockman — framed as rescuing a charitable trust.
- **Naming drama**: Musk wanted to call it "Freethink," Altman wanted "Axon" — in hindsight the creator quips "best name would have been ClosedAI."
- **2017 haunted mansion meeting**: Musk secretly pushed to convert OpenAI for-profit with himself as CEO and majority shareholder; when co-founders proposed equal equity, Brockman testified Musk "stood up and stormed around the table… grabbed the painting and started to storm out" before asking "When will you be departing from OpenAI?" — six months later Musk stopped donations and left.
- **Shivon Zilis**: mother of 4 of Elon's 14 children, was simultaneously on the OpenAI board and briefing Musk — became a significant conflict of interest when xAI launched.
- **November 2023 Altman firing**: Ilya Sutskever's memo accused Altman of a "consistent pattern of lying." Mira Murati temporarily replaced him. Altman's text to Murati ("Can you indicate directionally, good or bad?") yielded the now-immortal reply: **"Directionally, very bad."** Altman was reinstated days later after employee and investor pressure.
- **Satya Nadella's initial skepticism** about the Microsoft investment gave way to a $13B bet; his description of the November 2023 chaos: *"A sort of amateur city as far as I'm concerned."*
- **Case weakness**: Musk's own 2017 for-profit push, absence of a hard contract keeping OpenAI nonprofit, and his oath-admission that Grok stole OpenAI models all undermine his standing as a wronged charitable donor.
- **Judge**: Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers; jury is **advisory only**, so the outcome rests with her.
