## Summary
- Selling on "wave-ups" to rebuy at the next dip sounds smart but fails 4 out of 5 times: the market zooms back up before you can re-enter.
- The author abandoned this tactic after repeatedly being left out of major rallies, ending up with 30% returns instead of 300–500%.
- Students who urged selling on the wave also got burned when the market reversed in the opposite direction.

## Key Points
- **The wave-up temptation**: "After a rally, I'll sell and rebuy lower on the next dip."
- **The fatal flaw**: 4 out of 5 times after you exit, the market reverses *up* immediately, not down. You miss the biggest single-day moves.
- **Real cost**: Missing the 10 best trading days over 20 years can turn a 400% gain into a 50% loss.
- **The pattern**: Market drops → you panic → market zooms back → you're stuck on sidelines with only a 30% gain while holders compounded 400%.
- **Author's personal mistake**: Used to do this for years; gave up because it consistently underperformed simply holding.
- **The lesson**: The same logic applies to hedging and market timing—the opportunity cost of being wrong usually exceeds the benefit of being right.
