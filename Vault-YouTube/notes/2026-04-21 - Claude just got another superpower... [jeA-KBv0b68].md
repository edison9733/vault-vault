## Summary
- [[Anthropic]] launched **Claude Design**, an [[Opus 4.7]]-powered platform that converts Figma files and uploaded design systems into fully interactive prototypes, pitch decks, and production-ready UIs; [[Figma]] stock dropped 7% on announcement.
- [[Opus 4.7]] (released ~April 14, 2026) adds 3.75 MP vision and scores 87.6% on SWE-bench, but is slower than [[Google Stitch]] and Cursor Composer; community debate over whether it regresses versus 4.6.
- Claude Design outperforms [[Google Stitch]] on interactivity (working animations, shaders, full-length video animations), but iterations take 5–10 minutes each and design-system adherence is inconsistent.

## Key Points
- **Claude Design** is Anthropic's new UI/UX generation tool powered by [[Opus 4.7]], directly competing with [[Google Stitch]]; Figma stock dropped **7%** within hours of the announcement.
- **Opus 4.7** key specs: **3.75 MP** vision (up to **2,576 px** long edge), **87.6%** on SWE-bench (crushes Opus 4.6; still behind the unreleased "Mythos" model), described as "more tasteful and creative."
- Controversy: a vocal community segment claims Opus 4.7 is a **regression** on general tasks; a conspiracy theory alleges Anthropic deliberately degraded 4.6 over preceding months to inflate 4.7's apparent improvement on release day.
- **Interactivity advantage**: Claude Design produces fully interactive outputs with working animations and **shader-based effects** — the key differentiator over [[Google Stitch]], which handles static layouts better.
- **Design system integration**: upload via GitHub repo link or direct Figma file import; forces style consistency across all generated screens. In theory: design 2 screens, provide the Figma file, get 100 screens back.
- **Iteration workflow**: draw annotations directly on the canvas and attach a new prompt; Claude regenerates affected screens in 5–10 minutes per pass.
- ==Claude Design's ability to extrapolate 100 screens from a 2-screen Figma seed collapses the UI designer role to "prompt engineer" — the same identity displacement that hit software developers in 2023.==
