# DEX

**What it is** - DEX stands for "decentralized exchange." A trading platform that runs entirely as smart contracts on a blockchain, with no central company or server controlling it. Users trade directly with each other (or with a liquidity pool) through the contract's logic, without creating an account on a centralized service.

**Why it matters** - DEXs enable permissionless token trading: no central authority can freeze accounts, demand identity verification, or be shut down by regulators. They are a core building block of [[DeFi]] and use [[Escrow Program]] logic at their heart to hold assets during swaps and ensure both sides of a trade settle atomically.

**Example** - Uniswap is the largest DEX on Ethereum. A user swapping USDC for ETH deposits USDC into a smart contract pool; the contract calculates the exchange rate from the pool's liquidity and releases ETH — fully automated, no human intermediary, settlement in seconds.
