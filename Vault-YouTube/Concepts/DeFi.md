# DeFi

**What it is** - Short for Decentralized Finance. DeFi refers to financial services — trading, lending, borrowing, earning interest — that run on public blockchains instead of banks or exchanges. Anyone with an internet connection can participate without needing permission from a company.

**Why it matters** - DeFi removes the middleman (banks, brokers, exchanges) by replacing them with open-source code. Transactions settle on-chain, so the rules are transparent and no single party can freeze funds or change terms.

**Example** - A trader can swap tokens directly on a decentralized exchange like Raydium (built on [[Solana]]) without creating an account or trusting a centralized custodian. Solana co-founder Anatoly Yakovenko predicts "there will be no difference between DeFi and finance — we'll just call it all finance."
