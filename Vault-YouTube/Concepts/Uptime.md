# Uptime

**What it is** - The percentage of time a service is accessible and functioning correctly, usually measured over a rolling year. "Five nines" (99.999%) means less than ~5 minutes of downtime per year; "one nine" (90%) means up to 36 days of downtime per year.

**Why it matters** - For infrastructure that millions of people depend on daily, even small uptime gaps translate to large lost-productivity events. SLAs (Service Level Agreements) codify uptime commitments; missing them erodes user trust and may trigger financial penalties.

**Example** - AWS S3 guarantees eleven-nines of durability. By contrast, third-party monitoring measured [[GitHub]] uptime at ~86% in April 2026 — meaning GitHub was functionally unavailable for roughly 14% of the month, while its own status page still claimed >99%.
